Mini Triceratops

Pattern: Club Crochet by @louiesloops

This was a super fun pattern! I wanted to start with something small, coming back to attempting crochet after over 10 years. (I'd last tried in high school, but I could never quite grok turning, or how to tell which side was the "right" side!)

Having the video tutorial available definitely helped, especially with the more interesting bits like the bobble stitch legs and picot stitches in the frill that I hadn't done before. Big kudos to the videography at Club Crochet, as they're one of the clearest video tutorials I've seen. However, the pattern I think could benefit from a more liberal usage of parentheses. There are some parts that would have been ambiguous had I not consulted the video, for example when it says

I've found that making these out of a stiffer/scratchier yarn results in them having slightly longer snouts. I like being able to get slightly different looks, as it makes them feel more realistic as a group.

I've been making these more or less continuously since the first one since they're so CUTE and can work up in one evening. You can buy one on the StitchNSquish Etsy!
